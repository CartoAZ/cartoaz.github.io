UW Pepperell Lab GIS Analyst & Cartographer

Aug. 2015 - Sep. 2016 | Madison, WI

I worked on a phylogeography project investigating the spread of Mycobacterium tuberculosis along ancient trade routes in the the Pepperell Lab. I used Python 2.7 for cleaning trade route datasets and Numpy to calculate great circle distances along trade routes. I digitized ancient trade routes from scanned maps using ArcGIS 10.3. I performed network and other geospatial analyses using modern M. tuberculosis isolate locations and ancient trade routes to help determine if the spatial patterns of genetic variation were correlated to the geographic distance between isolates. For final publication, I created static maps with Adobe Illustrator.
